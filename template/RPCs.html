RPCs - Brief explanation

Having unsecured RPIs (Remote Procedure Calls) on your crypto wallet is risky because it increases the risk of your funds being stolen or compromised. An RPC is essentially a connection between your wallet and a remote server, allowing your wallet to interact with the Ethereum network and perform various actions, such as sending and receiving transactions. If an RPC is unsecured, it means that there is no encryption or authentication in place to protect the communication between your wallet and the remote server. This makes it easier for malicious actors to intercept and tamper with the communication, potentially stealing your private keys or compromising your transactions.

For example, an attacker could perform a man-in-the-middle attack, intercepting the communication between your wallet and the remote server and modifying the transactions before they reach the Ethereum network. This could result in your funds being sent to an unintended address, or the attacker could simply steal your funds altogether. In addition, an unsecured RPC could also be used as a way for attackers to gain access to sensitive information stored on your wallet, such as your private keys or seed phrase. To avoid these risks, it's important to only use RPIs that are secured with encryption and authentication, and to always verify the authenticity of the server before sending any sensitive information. Additionally, it's a good idea to regularly monitor the security of your RPIs and update them as needed to ensure that they remain secure.
